An event-driven pipeline moved ~2 TB a week across ~45 daily runs and failed about 40% of the time, so recovery was a daily chore and only ~60% of deliveries hit the 12-hour SLA. The shift that mattered was to stop trying to prevent every failure and make every stage safe to re-run: idempotent, keyed by capture timestamps and client identifiers, with distributed locks, bounded retries, and restart-from-failure checkpoints. End-to-end observability in Elastic and Sentry now surfaces issues in under two minutes.

end to end

The production data platform had to move from Azure to GCP while enterprise customers in Australia and the US kept getting data every day. A big-bang cutover would risk everyone at once, so I ran the GCP stack in parallel, compared outputs with canary A/B runs, and moved customers over one at a time with rollback at every step, onto Pub/Sub, Cloud Storage, Compute Engine, and GKE, with no missed delivery.
